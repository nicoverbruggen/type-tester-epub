Chapter Six: Verse and Line Breaks

Poem Specimen

One poem is included here because Chapter Six is less a reference chapter than a demonstration of how spacing, line length, and silence behave in verse.

ii. Tide Table

Low at six.

High at noon.

Low again before the lamps come on.

I wrote the times in my notebook

as though keeping track of something.

The sea kept no record.

The sea has never needed to.

Chapter Eight: Dialogue and Pace

Dialogue Specimen

Chapter Eight is useful less as a reference grid than as a test of conversational rhythm: short exchanges, pauses, and attribution that need to remain effortless on the page.

“You’ve been quiet,” Yvette said.

“I’m always quiet.”

“You’re unusually quiet. There’s a register of quiet you’ve been in all evening that I would describe as pointed.”

“I’ve been thinking about leaving,” she said.

“The firm?”

“The firm. The flat. The whole —” she gestured at the window — “arrangement. I might even move countries.”

“And go where, exactly?”

Tanya considered this. “I haven’t worked that out yet. Antwerp, possibly, in the first instance.”

Yvette looked at her. “Antwerp.”

“It has a typographic history. A serious one.”
